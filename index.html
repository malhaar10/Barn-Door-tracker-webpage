Barn Door Tracker

Minimal, accurate timing for a curved-bolt tracker with a single motor.

The Math

A Barn Door/Haig Mount counteracts Earth's rotation by opening two boards at a precise "Sidereal" rate. To track the stars, the mount must complete one full rotation every 23h 56m 4.09s.

The Sidereal Rate

The core of our calculation relies on the sidereal angular rate:

ω = 2π / 1436.07 ≈ 0.004375 rad/min

The Master Formula

To avoid "star trailing," your drive bolt must rotate at this speed:

T_rev = P / (R × ω)
  • T_rev: Minutes per full 360° turn of the bolt.
  • P: Thread Pitch (distance moved per turn).
  • R: Radius from the hinge center to the bolt center.

The Science

If you use a straight bolt, the geometry changes as the mount opens, causing tracking to slow down. Bending the rod into an arc with radius $L$ eliminates this error entirely.

Tip: Bend the rod around a jig slightly smaller than L to account for the rod's own thickness.
